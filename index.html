About What is a Julian Day?

Calendar systems are awkward for computation: months have different lengths, leap years shift dates, and comparing timestamps across centuries becomes messy. Astronomers sidestep this by counting time as a single continuous number — the total days elapsed since January 1, 4713 BC at 12:00 UTC. This is the Julian Day (JD).

Every higher-level calculation in this tool — local sidereal time, coordinate transforms, planetary positions — starts from JD. The well-known reference point is 2000 Jan 1, 12:00 UTC = JD 2451545.0, called the J2000.0 epoch. Enter that date above to verify.
